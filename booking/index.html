The signature difference

A 3D map that sells the venue itself.

“Daybed — $150” is a row in a grid, and grids get compared on price. The Clubtech booking map sells the venue instead: guests drift over a 3D birds-eye view, tap the exact piece of furniture they want, and commit to the experience before the price appears.

  • Zones explored from overhead — front row, swim-up, DJ booth
  • 360° walkthroughs of the exact spot before checkout
  • Emotional commitment first, price reveal second
  • The same map the team runs service from

It sells whatever the venue sells: daybeds for a beach club, VIP booths and bottle service for a nightclub floor.

Tiered packages

A pricing ladder in every zone.

Bed only, party package, ultimate experience. The guest who came for a $150 daybed sees — in the same view — what $400 turns the day into, at the moment of highest intent instead of a server’s pitch at 2pm.

  • Tiers anchored to the zone: front row carries a front-row minimum
  • Guests compare your options against each other, not the venue down the beach
  • The operator sets the rungs; the map does the selling

Upsells & add-ons

Revenue on top of the reservation.

Once the spot is chosen, add-ons stack onto the booking in the same flow — bottles, celebration cakes, transfers, products. One tap each, attached to the reservation, paid with it.

  • The guest builds the day themselves — no upsell conversation on the floor
  • Sells 24/7: the birthday group adds the cake at midnight on Tuesday
  • The cheapest revenue a venue earns — acquisition already paid at booking

Dynamic pricing

Make more from the same seats.

The same daybed is not worth the same amount on a sold-out Saturday and a slow Tuesday — but on most rate cards it costs the same anyway. Clubtech prices furniture to demand, daypart, and remaining inventory, flexing rates automatically inside rules the operator sets.

  • Peak inventory captures what it is actually worth
  • Off-peak inventory finds the price at which it fills
  • Yield, not gouging — more from the same seats without adding one

FAQ

Questions operators ask.

How does an online booking system for a beach club work?

+

Guests open the venue’s own website, pick an exact daybed, cabana, or table on an interactive map, choose a package tier, stack add-ons like bottles or transfers, and prepay. The venue banks the revenue days ahead, the reservation syncs to the floor plan in real time, and every guest in the party enters the marketing database before arrival.

What is an interactive venue booking map?

+

It is a live model of the venue guests book from directly. On Clubtech it is a 3D birds-eye map: guests explore zones from overhead, tap the specific piece of furniture they want, and watch 360° walkthroughs of the spot before the price appears. They commit to the experience first, then pay for it — instead of choosing a row from a grid.

How do venues sell add-ons online?

+

Inside the booking flow, as stackable one-tap options attached to the reservation — bottles, celebration cakes, transfers, products. Because the guest has already committed to the spot, attaching spend takes seconds, and the add-on store sells 24/7 whether or not the venue is open. It is revenue on top of the reservation, collected before the guest arrives.

What is dynamic pricing for venues?

+

Pricing the same furniture differently as demand, daypart, and remaining inventory move — the way airlines price seats. A front-row daybed on a sold-out Saturday should not cost what it costs on a quiet Tuesday. Clubtech flexes rates automatically within rules the operator sets, so peak days yield more and slow days still fill.

Can guests book a beach club or nightclub on mobile?

+

They already do — on Clubtech, 82% of bookings happen on a phone after 10pm. The checkout is built for that moment: sub-second load, no app install, Apple Pay and Google Pay, and a confirmed, prepaid reservation in four taps. If checkout needs a laptop, the venue is losing the guests who decide in the group chat.

Can a venue booking system sell event tickets too?

+

Yes. Clubtech includes a native events booking widget alongside furniture reservations, with tiered tickets and QR-code check-in at the door. Ticket buyers land in the same guest database as daybed and table bookers, so an event crowd becomes an audience the venue can market the rest of the season to.
