Venue operations software runs the service day for beach clubs, day clubs, nightclubs, and hotel pools: a live internal floor plan, automatic seating allocation, real-time inventory, guest-list management, and QR check-in in one system. On Clubtech, the floor plan and the online booking map are the same object — so before the doors open, the GM already knows which furniture is booked, prepaid, and spoken for.

The floor plan is the booking map

Guests book from a 3D birds-eye interactive map of the venue — real zones, exact furniture. Internally, that same map is your floor plan. Not an export of it, not a copy updated overnight: the same object, viewed from the operator's side.

That single design decision removes the most common failure in front-of-house software — version drift. When the booking system and the floor plan are separate, every online sale is a pending edit someone has to carry across, and every hour of lag is a window where the floor team is working from fiction. On Clubtech, online sales sync to the internal floor plan in real time. A cabana sold at 11:42am is on the floor plan at 11:42am, with the package attached.

The floor team stops asking "is this list current?" because there is only one list, and it is always current.

Automatic seating allocation

The 6pm whiteboard session — a manager, a printout, and forty parties to place before service — is the daily tax venues pay for keeping bookings and the floor in separate systems. Clubtech pays it for you: automatic seating allocation places confirmed parties onto the live floor plan as bookings land.

Instead of building the floor from scratch every afternoon, the team reviews a floor that is already placed. The hours that went into allocation go back into service — briefing hosts on the big packages, walking the zones, working the arrivals. And because allocation runs continuously, the late Friday-night booking for Saturday doesn't trigger a redo; it simply appears, placed.

Real-time inventory sync

Double-sells have one cause: two channels selling from two lists. The website sells daybed 14, the host stand sells daybed 14 to a walk-up, and at 1pm two parties are standing over the same furniture while a manager improvises a refund.

Clubtech runs every channel off one inventory, synced in real time. You set what the team actually sells — the furniture, the zones, the dayparts, the packages on each — and online and walk-up draw down the same pool. When a sunbed sells online, it is gone at the host stand in the same moment; because both channels read the same pool, nothing can be sold twice. No buffer stock held back "just in case," no spreadsheet reconciliation, no apology round at 1pm.

The service-day console

The operator console is where the service day starts: which furniture is prepaid, what each party ordered, and where tonight's revenue is sitting — by zone, by daypart, before the first guest arrives. A host greeting a party already knows they booked the party package with two extra bottles; a floor manager can see at a glance which zones are sold through and which need attention.

This page covers the operating side deliberately. The four levers that grow the number the console shows — prepayments, upsells, dynamic pricing, and retargeting — live in the booking journey, and the reports that read the patterns behind it live in guest intelligence. Operations is where those numbers become a floor you can actually run.

Guest list and visitor management

The door is the last part of most venues still running on paper — a clipboard, five promoters' phones, and a bouncer's judgment call. Clubtech's guest-list and visitor management puts the door on the same system as the floor: table bookings, guest-list registrations, and comps in one view, so the door team confirms a name in seconds instead of triaging three lists.

The quieter win is what the list becomes afterward. Free-entry registrations capture contact details as guests sign up, so the guest list stops evaporating at close and starts building a marketable database — the same database every paid booking already feeds. For the full door-and-VIP picture, see how nightclub table booking absorbs the DM-and-doorman era.

QR-code check-in

For events and ticketed nights, Clubtech's events and ticketing runs QR-code check-in at the door. Each ticket carries its own code; the door scans, the system confirms, and the line moves. No printed manifests, no name-by-name lookups, no counterfeit judgment calls at midnight.

Check-in also closes the data loop on the busiest nights: the door works from confirmed tickets instead of a printout — and when furniture or tickets sell out, priority-entry capture keeps taking details, turning a full house into next event's audience.

Questions operators ask

What software do venues use to manage the floor?

Most venues run a patchwork: a reservations tool, a spreadsheet for allocation, a clipboard at the door, and a POS that talks to none of them. Venue operations software like Clubtech replaces the patchwork with one system — the booking map, the floor plan, the guest list, and inventory are the same live object, so what sells online is exactly what the team sees on the floor.

How does automatic seating allocation work?

Every confirmed booking is placed onto the live floor plan automatically, so parties have furniture assigned before service starts instead of being dealt out in a pre-shift huddle. The team reviews a placed floor rather than building one from scratch — and because the plan updates as online sales land, the picture stays current right up to doors.

How do venues stop double-booking sunbeds and tables?

Run every channel off one inventory. Double-sells happen when online booking and walk-up sales draw from different lists that reconcile too late. On Clubtech, online sales sync to the internal floor plan in real time and the floor team sells from that same live inventory — when a daybed is gone, it is gone everywhere at once.

Can the door team see online bookings in real time?

Yes. Online bookings, guest-list registrations, and ticket sales land in the same system the door works from, updating in real time. The door team sees confirmed, prepaid parties as they book — not a printout from 6pm — and QR-code check-in confirms tickets at the line without radioing the office.

Does Clubtech replace the guest list clipboard?

Yes. Guest-list and visitor management is built into the platform, so the door works from one system instead of a clipboard plus promoters' phones. Free-entry registrations capture contact details as guests sign up, which means the list finally builds a marketable database instead of evaporating at close.
