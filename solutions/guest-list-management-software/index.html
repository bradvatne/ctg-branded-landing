The guest list management system that works the door and the database.

A guest list management system that captures every guest at the door, checks them in by QR, and turns the list into a marketable database — not a spreadsheet.

Book a Demo See the Platform

It's 11:40pm and the door is a bottleneck. A host is scrolling a phone with four promoter lists open, a printout is smudged from the drizzle, and a group of six is arguing that their names are "definitely on it." Somewhere behind the rope, a prepaid table sits empty because nobody at the door can see it. The guest list — the thing that's supposed to control who gets in — is the slowest, least reliable object in the building.

Most guest list management systems fix only half of that. They speed up the door and forget the database. For a premium venue the guest list is not a spreadsheet you check names off: it's the door, plus every-guest data capture, plus priority entry, plus QR check-in — and it should live inside the booking platform, not beside it. Clubtech is that system, built for the operator: reservations, front-of-house operations, and marketing in one platform, processing $332k in weekly GMV for venue partners in more than 7 countries.

The door, run from one screen

Guest list and visitor management (VMS) replaces the clipboard, the printout, and the five promoters' phones with a single live list. The host at the rope works from what the operator console already knows.

  • One list, not five. Reservations, tickets, priority registrations, and promoter names arrive in the same system, so the door isn't reconciling sources under pressure.
  • QR-code check-in. For events and ticketing, guests arrive with a QR code and are checked in with a tap — no name-hunting, no duplicate entries, no "spell that again."
  • The floor in view at the door. Because the booking map and the internal floor plan are the same object, the host can see which tables are prepaid and who they belong to. A confirmed party gets greeted by name instead of triaged in a queue of maybes.

The operator console shows the night before doors open: what's booked, who's coming, where tonight's revenue is sitting. The door stops being a guess.

Every guest, not just the booker

This is where a guest management system earns its place. Most tools capture the person who paid and lose everyone else — so a table of eight becomes one email address, and the other seven guests, who came, drank, and might come back, are invisible.

On Clubtech, the booker invites their group, and each invited guest supplies their own details. The venue captures the whole party, not just the payer. Eight guests, eight records — a marketable database built by the booking itself, without a signup sheet at the door or a QR poster nobody scans.

That's the difference between a list you check names against and a list that grows your marketing every night you open.

Sold out is not a lost guest

When furniture sells out, the guest list keeps working. Priority and free-entry data capture takes the guest's details anyway — the person who wanted in this Saturday is the easiest sell for the next one.

  • Priority entry. Guests register for the door even when there's nothing left to book, so demand you can't seat still becomes contact you can market to.
  • Free-entry capture. The names that used to evaporate at the rope now enter the same database as your paying guests.

Sold out ≠ lost guest. A generic RSVP tool treats a full night as a closed door; a proper guest management system treats it as a full inbox for next weekend.

From list to lookalike

A guest list that just controls the door is a cost. A guest list wired into your marketing is an asset. Clubtech turns the check-in log into guest intelligence: more than 20 built-in reports — booking volume, lead time by daypart, average value by package, repeat-customer share — with no CSV exports to wrangle.

That data drives the ads loop. Bookings fire to Meta, Google, and GA4 in real time with revenue posted back, so campaigns optimize toward booked dollars, not clicks. Lookalike audiences are seeded from your highest-LTV bookers — prospecting is modeled on your best guests, not page likers. And because 82% of bookings happen on a phone after 10pm, the whole flow is mobile-first: sub-second load, no app install, Apple Pay and Google Pay, four taps. See the platform overview, or the operations view for how the service day runs.

Beyond the standalone list app

A standalone list app or a spreadsheet does one thing: it holds names. It doesn't sell the table, capture the group, allocate the floor, or feed your ads. So the door runs on one tool, the reservations on another, and the marketing on a third — and the guest data never lives in one place. Clubtech is one platform where the nightclub table booking map, the day club booking system, the door, and the database are the same object. It sits on top of your existing POS and PMS with no major IT changes — it connects the parts that used to be blind to each other.

Questions operators ask

What is a guest list management system?

It's the software that controls who enters your venue and captures who they are. A basic one holds names and checks them off; a premium one runs the door with QR check-in, captures every guest in a party rather than just the booker, and stores the data where your marketing can use it. On Clubtech the guest list lives inside the booking platform, so the door and the database are never out of sync.

What should guest list management software do beyond holding names?

It should turn the door into data. Good guest list management software captures every guest's details — not just the payer's — checks them in by QR, and feeds the whole party into a guest database you own. It should also keep working when you sell out, capturing priority and free-entry registrations so a full night still grows your list.

How is a guest management system different from an online guest list or RSVP tool?

An online guest list or RSVP tool collects names for one event and stops there. A guest management system runs the door in real time, ties each guest to bookings and revenue, allocates the floor, and pushes that data into your ads and reporting. The list becomes a marketable asset instead of a one-night attendance sheet.

Can a guest list management system capture data for a whole group, not just the booker?

Yes — and it should. On Clubtech the booker invites their group and each invited guest supplies their own details, so a table of eight produces eight records. That's the difference between a spreadsheet that captures one email and a database that grows with every party you seat.

Does the guest list connect to check-in and the floor plan?

On Clubtech, yes — they're the same system. QR check-in marks guests off the live list, the operator floor plan shows which tables are prepaid and who they belong to, and the door works from one screen instead of five promoter phones. Standalone list apps leave check-in, seating, and the guest record in three separate tools.
