The operating case

Inventory · revenue · service day · owned data

A beach club does not need one flat price for every date, daypart, and position. It needs a booking system that can distinguish the front row from the shade line, attach the right package, and show the operator what has committed before doors open.

For Phuket operators, this page focuses on that pricing-and-demand job. It does not rely on unsupported local season, payment, or guest-behavior statistics.

High season sells. Green season captures.

Pre-sell the premium weekends, then keep sold-out and priority demand working when inventory changes.

How it works

Make the deck bookable by zone and demand

See the booking flow

  1. 1
    Map the furniture and zones. Guests choose a specific daybed, cabana, table, or area from the 3D birds-eye view.
  2. 2
    Build the package ladder. Bed-only and higher-value packages can carry approved add-ons inside the flow.
  3. 3
    Set pricing by demand. Rates can vary by date, daypart, and inventory rather than remaining flat.
  4. 4
    Take prepayment. Deposits, prepayments, and minimum spends commit revenue before arrival.

Where it fits

International checkout, venue-owned data

Clubtech supports multi-currency booking and keeps the booking under the venue's own brand. Exact payment methods and integration scope are confirmed for each venue during implementation.

Every-guest capture and 20+ reports turn the booking log into an owned audience and a clearer view of demand.

The proof

Southeast Asian product proof, stated narrowly

FINNS Beach Club in Bali is Clubtech's named public proof point. Its CEO describes moving from on-the-day bookings with no financial guarantee to millions of dollars in pre-paid bookings each month.

That proves the booking and revenue mechanics in a Southeast Asian beach-club operation. It does not claim a Phuket customer, a Phuket result, or a local market statistic.

Operator questions

Questions operators ask

Can Clubtech price different dates and zones differently?

Yes. Dynamic pricing can vary by demand, daypart, and inventory, so a premium position and a quieter date do not need the same rate.

Does Clubtech support multi-currency booking?

Yes. The exact payment methods and connector scope are confirmed for the venue during implementation.

Who owns the guest data?

The venue. Clubtech is white-label: the booking runs on the venue's domain and design, and the guest data belongs to the venue.

Does Clubtech publish Phuket customer results?

No Phuket-specific customer result is claimed on this page. The named public proof is FINNS Beach Club in Bali.
