The beach club booking system built for Phuket's high season

A beach club booking system built for Phuket — map-first daybed sales, multi-currency checkout, pre-paid weekends. Proven in Southeast Asia.

Book a Demo See the Platform

Phuket sells more beach afternoons than almost anywhere in Southeast Asia, and it sells them to the most mixed guest list in the region — Australians on a week off, Europeans on a three-week escape, Gulf families in the summer, Chinese and Indian groups year-round, and a large expat crowd that treats Bang Tao like a local. A beach club booking system for Phuket has to do three things at once: sell specific furniture to guests planning from a phone the night before, take money in whatever way that guest's wallet works, and smooth a season that swings hard between the dry-season peak and the green-season lull.

Most venues on the island still run this through Instagram DMs, a WhatsApp number, and a reservation book. That works until the first sold-out Saturday of high season — which is exactly when it costs the most.

Why Phuket beach clubs outgrow DMs and walk-ups

The demand is already there. Guests search a venue name plus "booking" or "day bed price" before they've left the hotel breakfast buffet. What they land on decides whether that intent converts tonight or evaporates into a competitor's feed.

  • Guests decide late, on phones. Across Clubtech venues, 82% of bookings happen on a phone after 10pm — a couple on a Kata balcony planning tomorrow, a group chat settling on Bang Tao at midnight. A DM funnel answers in the morning; by then the plan has moved. Clubtech loads in under a second and takes a guest from venue map to paid confirmation in four taps, no app install.
  • The spot is the product. Phuket guests aren't buying entry, they're buying the front-row bed facing the Andaman, the swim-up corner, the cabana with shade for the kids. An interactive venue map with 360° walkthroughs lets them commit to their spot before the price appears — and by checkout they've stopped comparing venues.
  • Walk-up-only pricing leaves the upgrade behind. Tiered packages — bed only, party package, ultimate — with stackable add-ons like bottles, cakes, and hotel transfers sell the upgrade at checkout, when the guest is excited, not on the deck when they're settled in.

High season is a yield game, green season is a capture game

Phuket's calendar has two very different jobs for a booking system.

From roughly November to April, the island is full and the constraint is furniture. The question isn't whether Saturday sells — it's at what price, how far in advance, and how much of the deck went for walk-up rates when it could have pre-sold higher. Pre-payment and "Book Online & Save" pull commitment forward: guests lock in days ahead for a saving, you bank the revenue and see real capacity data before the weekend exists. Dynamic pricing then earns more from the same beds on the dates that deserve it. A no-show against a prepaid booking costs you nothing; a no-show against a penciled-in DM costs the whole daypart.

May to October, the job flips. Demand thins, storms reshuffle plans by lunchtime, and the venues that win are the ones that capture every guest who shows intent. When a zone sells out — or a guest browses and bails — that contact isn't lost: priority-entry capture takes the details anyway, and abandoned-booking events fire to Meta within seconds so ads return the guest to the exact bed, date, and price they left. In a market where the same traveler is on the island for another two weeks, that capture is next Tuesday's booking.

Every booking also fires to Meta, Google, and GA4 in real time with revenue attached, so campaigns optimize toward the guests who buy cabanas, not the ones who click cheapest. For a Phuket venue whose next hundred guests are currently scrolling in Sydney, Singapore, and Shanghai, that loop is the growth engine.

A checkout for every passport

Phuket's guest mix is the payment problem in miniature: one Saturday's front row can hold six nationalities. Clubtech checkout is multi-currency, with Apple Pay and Google Pay alongside international cards — so the guest booking from a Melbourne departure gate and the one booking from a Kamala villa close in the same four taps. The platform runs white-label throughout: your domain, your design, your guest data. A guest never sees our name — they see a faster version of your brand.

The commercials fit an island where margins live in the season: no monthly fee, a one-time $2,000 setup, and a 4% online processing fee paid by the customer. The platform earns when your deck sells, not before.

The dynamics that made that work — late mobile bookers, an international guest mix, weather that punishes walk-up-only revenue — are Phuket's dynamics too. The mechanics are laid out in the Bali page, and most of them transfer across the Andaman unchanged.

Bang Tao, Kamala, Patong — different strips, different inventory

Phuket's beaches don't sell the same product, and the booking system shouldn't pretend they do. The Bang Tao and Layan strip sells the premium day — big-format venues, bottle pre-sales, group bookings that need tiered packages out of the box. Kamala and Surin sell the sunset — front-row scarcity that a map-first flow sells out first instead of last. Patong and Kata sell energy and volume, where four-tap checkout and walk-up-versus-online price spreads move the most revenue. One platform, configured per venue — with 20+ built-in reports showing lead time by daypart, average value by package, and repeat-guest share, so the strip you're on is a pricing input, not a guess.

Questions operators ask

How do guests book beach clubs in Phuket?

Increasingly online and in advance: guests search the venue name plus "booking," open the venue's page on a phone, pick a specific daybed or cabana on an interactive map, add packages, and prepay. On Clubtech that flow is four taps with Apple Pay, Google Pay, or card — no app install, no DM thread, confirmation in seconds.

Can guests pay in their own currency?

Yes. Clubtech checkout supports multi-currency alongside Apple Pay, Google Pay, and international cards, which matters in a market where one weekend's guest list spans Australia, Europe, the Gulf, and East Asia. Guests pay the way they already pay at home, so the booking closes at the moment of intent instead of stalling at a payment screen.

What does a beach club booking system cost in Phuket?

Clubtech has no monthly fee. There's a one-time $2,000 setup per venue — covering widget setup, system configuration, and staff training — and a 4% online processing fee paid by the customer, not the venue. That structure means the platform only earns when it's selling your furniture, which suits a market with a pronounced low season.

Does prepayment work in a walk-up market like Phuket?

Yes, when it's paired with a reason to commit. An online-versus-walk-up price spread makes booking ahead read as a saving rather than a toll, and prepaid guests stop costing you no-shows. Premium zones — front rows, swim-ups, cabanas — pre-sell first; walk-ups still fill the rest of the deck at door rates.

How does a booking system help in green season?

By capturing demand instead of just serving it. Priority-entry capture takes guest details even when plans wash out, abandoned bookings retarget within seconds through Meta's Conversions API, and every booking feeds lookalike audiences of your highest-value guests. Low season becomes the period where the database grows, so high season opens with an audience already built.
