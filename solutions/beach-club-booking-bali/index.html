The operating case

Inventory · revenue · service day · owned data

Bali is where Clubtech has its clearest public beach-club proof. FINNS Beach Club runs its booking journey on Clubtech, moving from on-the-day bookings with no financial guarantee to millions of dollars in pre-paid bookings each month, in CEO Beau Whittington's account.

This page applies that proven operating model to the inventory a Bali beach club actually sells: a specific daybed, cabana, table, zone, package, and daypart.

Built for the booking made after 10pm.

Map-first mobile booking, local payments, and attributed revenue fit the way Bali weekends are decided.

How it works

Clubtech · Bali

Across the booking journey

One booking. Eight commercial jobs.

Explore the revenue mechanics

  • 1
    Sell the experience before the price. A 3D birds-eye map turns the venue into the product. Guests see the pool edge, the view, the shade, and the exact daybed or cabana they are buying.
  • 2
    Grow the order while intent is high. A TikTok-style mobile shop puts bottle videos and packages inside the journey. It has driven an 80% increase in bottle add-ons across the product.
  • 3
    Make payment feel instant. Multi-currency checkout runs through Stripe, with Apple Pay and Google Pay for one-tap wallet approval. The complete booking journey takes four taps.
  • 4
    Pull cash and certainty forward. Prepayments, deposits, and minimum spends secure the furniture before arrival, giving the venue predictable working float and a clearer capacity forecast.
  • 5
    Know the whole party. The booker invites the group and each guest supplies their own details. A booking for eight can create eight owned guest profiles, not one payer record.
  • 6
    Recover the booking that nearly happened. WhatsApp and email abandoned-booking journeys return the guest to the exact zone, date, and price they left.
  • 7
    Show marketing what revenue looks like. Paid bookings post back to Meta, Google Ads, TikTok, and GA4 with their value attached, helping the venue find and build audiences around its highest-spending guests.
  • 8
    Turn patterns into decisions. More than 20 reports show lead time, product mix, origin markets, repeat share, and revenue by hour, while the same live inventory runs allocation, the operator console, and the service day.

Operator questions

Questions operators ask

What booking system does FINNS Beach Club use?

FINNS Beach Club uses Clubtech for its booking journey. Its CEO has publicly described the move from on-the-day bookings with no financial guarantee to millions of dollars in pre-paid bookings each month.

Can guests choose a specific daybed or cabana?

Yes. The 3D birds-eye booking map lets guests select exact furniture or zones before choosing packages and paying.

Does Clubtech keep the booking under the venue's brand?

Yes. Clubtech is white-label: the booking runs on the venue's domain and design, and the guest data belongs to the venue.

How are Clubtech commercials scoped?

Commercials follow discovery. We map the venue, inventory, payments, integrations, teams, and rollout, then return with a proposal built around the confirmed operation. See how commercial scoping works.
