A beach club doesn't lose money at the door. It loses money in the gap between a guest deciding to come and a guest actually paying — the DM that never converts, the daybed held on a promise, the front row allocated by whoever texted the manager first. A $450 daybed that walks at 2pm is $450 you never see again.

A beach club booking system closes that gap. Guests choose the exact daybed, cabana, or table on a 3D birds-eye map of your venue, pick a package, and prepay — days before doors open. Clubtech is that system: reservations, front-of-house operations, and marketing in one platform, white-label on your own domain, processing $332k in weekly GMV across venue partners in more than 7 countries. The best beach clubs don't hope for a full Saturday. They sell it in advance.

The booking flow that sells the premium zones first

  • Guests buy the spot, not "a booking." They open the interactive map on their phone, explore zones — front row, swim-up, VIP — and watch 360° walkthroughs before they commit. Emotional commitment arrives before the price does, which is why the highest-margin furniture sells out first instead of last.
  • Packages do the upselling. Tiered options — bed only, party package, ultimate — with stackable add-ons like bottles, cakes, and transfers. The upgrade is one tap at booking, not a negotiation on the deck.
  • Payment happens at booking. Apple Pay, Google Pay, or card; four taps; sub-second load; no app install. Since 82% of bookings happen on a phone after 10pm, the flow is built for a guest on a sofa at 11:15 on a Tuesday — because that's where your Saturday is actually decided.

Four revenue levers, pulled before doors open

Clubtech captures revenue at every touchpoint, not just the moment of booking:

  1. Prepayments and deposits. Money down means the party arrives or the revenue stays with you. No-shows stop being a line item.
  2. Upsells and add-ons. Bottles, cakes, and transfers sold inside the booking flow — incremental spend collected 24/7, at the moment of maximum enthusiasm.
  3. Dynamic pricing. Saturday front row and Wednesday shade line are different products. Pricing to demand earns more from the same furniture.
  4. Abandoned-booking retargeting. A guest who browsed a date and left gets pulled back by dynamic ads showing the exact zone, date, and price they abandoned — the event fires within seconds.

Minimum spends slot into the same architecture: attached to specific furniture and dayparts, set at booking with prepayment behind them, so the minimum is a guarantee rather than a doorstep argument. The revenue overview breaks down each lever.

Sold out is a list, not a dead end

When the map shows full, most booking flows show a wall. Clubtech captures the guest anyway: priority and free-entry registration takes contact details even when there's no furniture left to sell. Sold-out Saturdays quietly build the audience for the ones that aren't — and because the whole party enters the database, not just the booker, your marketable audience grows faster than your booking count.

Bookings that feed your ads

Every paid booking fires to Meta, Google, and GA4 in real time, with revenue values posted back. In practice: lookalike audiences seeded from your highest-value bookers rather than page likers, retargeting pools for map browsers who didn't buy, and campaigns that optimize toward booked dollars instead of clicks. Meanwhile, more than 20 built-in reports turn the reservation log into guest intelligence — revenue by hour, lead time by daypart, product mix, repeat share — without a CSV export in sight.

Questions operators ask

What is a beach club booking system?

A beach club booking system lets guests choose and pay for specific daybeds, cabanas, sunbeds, and VIP tables online before arrival — on Clubtech, from a 3D birds-eye map of the venue. Beyond reservations it handles tiered packages, prepayment, minimum spends, floor operations, and marketing data, turning the deck from first-come seating into pre-sold inventory.

How much does a beach club booking system cost?

Clubtech has no monthly fee. There's a 4% online processing fee paid by the customer and a $2,000 one-time setup per venue, which covers widget setup, system configuration, and staff training. That structure means the platform's revenue scales with your bookings rather than sitting on your P&L as fixed software cost.

How do you increase beach club revenue with a booking system?

Pre-sell the venue instead of waiting for walk-ups. The four levers that move fastest: prepayments and deposits that eliminate no-shows, upsells and add-ons sold inside the booking flow, dynamic pricing that prices furniture to demand, and abandoned-booking retargeting that recovers guests who browsed and left. Capturing contact details when you're sold out compounds all four.

Can one system handle sunbeds, daybeds, cabanas, and VIP tables?

Yes. On a map-based system every furniture type is a zone on the same venue map, each with its own pricing, packages, and minimum spend. Guests compare the front row to the swim-up and self-select — which is why mixed-inventory venues see premium zones sell out first rather than last.

Is Clubtech a marketplace?

No — Clubtech is white-label software the venue owns. Bookings happen on your domain, in your design, and every guest lands in your database, not a portal's. Marketplaces resell your inventory next to your competitors and keep the guest relationship; a booking system on your own site keeps the margin and the audience.

How does minimum spend work at a beach club?

A minimum spend is a committed food-and-beverage amount attached to premium furniture — a cabana might carry one number on Saturday and another midweek. Set at booking with prepayment or a deposit behind it, the minimum is guaranteed before the guest arrives, protecting yield on the furniture that earns it without taxing the shade line.
