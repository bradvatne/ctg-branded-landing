Megatix or Clubtech: ticketing and furniture are different jobs

Megatix is a white-label ticketing platform with organizer-owned customer data. Clubtech adds exact-furniture commerce, packages, dayparts, and floor operations.

What Clubtech owns

Events and ticketing

The full product explanation lives on the canonical capability page. This comparison stays focused on fit.

See the capability ↗

A beach club often runs two products at once. The headline event sells entry. The normal trading day sells daybeds, cabanas, tables, packages, and time on the deck. Megatix is built around the first transaction. Clubtech covers ticketing and also carries exact furniture through booking, pricing, and floor operations.

The right answer may be one platform or a clean split between the two.

Best fit for Megatix

Megatix is a strong fit for event organizers that need self-service ticket creation, a white-label website widget, ticket scanning, point-of-sale tools, affiliate tracking, reporting, and customer-data ownership. Its current official organizer page states that organizers can sell from their own websites and legally own their customer data.

For festivals, concerts, attractions, and one-off venue events, that is a credible, focused proposition.

Where Clubtech fits

Clubtech fits when the event sits inside a venue that also sells physical inventory every day. Guests choose the exact daybed or table, add the relevant package, prepay, and arrive against a live floor plan. Ticketed entry can run alongside that workflow without reducing the cabana to a ticket type.

See Clubtech events and ticketing, the day-club workflow, and service-day check-in.

Coexistence and switching considerations

Keeping Megatix for major ticket onsales and Clubtech for daily furniture can be sensible. Set one owner for each allocation and decide where combined packages live. A "ticket plus reserved daybed" offer should not deduct capacity from two disconnected systems without a reconciliation rule.

If considering a full switch, test ticket creation, high-volume entry, refunds, promoter reporting, furniture selection, package setup, and floor allocation. Confirm current payment methods and market availability directly with both vendors.

Product and next-step pathway

Start under Revenue: events and ticketing for the commercial setup, then Bookings: check-in for the door. If the event also sells cabanas or tables, review the day-club solution and book a demo.

Questions operators ask

Does Megatix let organizers own customer data?

Yes. Its current official organizer page explicitly describes selling from the organizer's own site and owning the customer data.

When is Clubtech the stronger fit?

When a venue needs ticketing and exact-furniture commerce in the same operating model: map selection, packages, dayparts, prepayment, live allocation, and booking attribution.

Can Megatix and Clubtech coexist?

Yes. Megatix can own event tickets while Clubtech owns daily furniture, as long as combined inventory and check-in responsibilities are explicit.

Test the event and the normal day

Do not evaluate only the headline night. Book a demo to see the ticket, furniture, and floor workflows together.
