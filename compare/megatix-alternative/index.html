The Megatix alternative for venues that sell furniture, not just tickets

Megatix sells the ticket — white-label, own-your-data, strong across Southeast Asia. Clubtech sells the daybed: a spatial map, packages, dayparts, prepaid minimums. Here's how to choose.

Book a Demo See the Platform

If you run events in Bali, Phuket, or anywhere across Southeast Asia, you've probably crossed paths with Megatix — it moves tickets for some of the region's biggest festivals and nightlife brands. But a beach club or day club has two businesses running under one roof. There's the occasional ticketed event — the foam party, the sunset session with a headline DJ — and there's the daily business of selling furniture: daybeds, cabanas, minimum spends, and packages to a guest who wants a specific spot in the sun this Saturday. Megatix is built for the first. The venues searching for a Megatix alternative are usually trying to solve the second — and discovering that a ticket engine and a furniture engine are not the same machine.

What Megatix is

Megatix is an Australian-owned, self-service event ticketing and event-management platform, headquartered in Perth and strong across Australia, New Zealand, and Southeast Asia — Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Vietnam. It powers ticketing for large festivals and nightlife events, from Djakarta Warehouse Project to the Perth Royal Show, and it has a genuine footprint in the region's beach-club and nightclub event scene.

Its positioning is sharp and, frankly, good: Megatix is a technology provider, not a marketplace. Organisers sell tickets from their own site through a white-label booking engine and legally own 100% of their customer data — a real differentiator against the incumbents that rent your audience back to you. The feature set is a mature ticketing stack: one-off and recurring events, access control and scanning through the Megatix app, box-office POS, affiliate and promoter tracking, localized payments including buy-now-pay-later, high-volume onsale handling for surge drops, and integrations for analytics, CRM, and resale.

For selling entry to an event — a night, a festival, a one-off party — Megatix is a strong, purpose-built choice. This page is for the venues whose core product isn't a ticket.

Tickets and tiers vs furniture and dayparts

The unit of sale shapes the whole product. Megatix grew up selling entry to an event: ticket types with price tiers, recurring sessions, high-volume onsales, scan-in at the door. When a beach club uses it, the venue becomes an event — you buy a ฿1,800 or ฿25,000 ticket to the foam party, not a specific lounger by the water.

A beach club or day club transaction inverts that. Most of the money isn't a ticket to an event — it's a guest, found on Instagram, deciding on a phone screen whether a specific daybed is worth $450 this Saturday afternoon. What that guest needs is a storefront, not a ticket queue:

  • A map the guest drives. On Clubtech, guests open a 3D birds-eye interactive map, explore zones, watch 360° walkthroughs, and tap the exact bed or cabana they want. Emotional commitment happens before the price appears — which is why premium rows sell first online instead of being allocated at the door.
  • A package ladder, not a ticket tier. Bed only, bed plus minimum spend, party package, ultimate experience — with bottles, cakes, and transfers stacked at checkout as one-tap add-ons. Upsell revenue is captured while the card is out, 24/7.
  • Dayparts and demand pricing. Morning and afternoon sessions on the same furniture, sunset premiums, event-day rates — dynamic pricing so the same deck earns more on Saturday than Tuesday. The day club model runs entirely on this arithmetic.
  • Prepayment as the default. Deposits and prepaid zone minimums days ahead, killing no-shows and turning the weekend from a walk-up guess into pre-sold certainty — plus priority-entry capture when furniture sells out, so a full deck still grows the database.

Megatix will happily sell tickets to a beach club's event nights, and it does that well. But there is no spatial floor plan where a guest picks unit by unit, no cabana-by-cabana inventory, no bottle-service table reservation running as the daily engine. That's the gap a furniture-first venue feels most.

Distribution: the ticket queue vs the ads loop

Both platforms are white-label and both let you own your customer data — Megatix deserves real credit here, because that ownership stance is exactly right. The difference is what happens after the sale, and where the next guest comes from.

Day venues buy their demand from Meta and Google, and that machine needs specific plumbing. On Clubtech, the booking is the conversion event: every reservation fires to Meta, Google, and GA4 in real time with its revenue value posted back, so campaigns optimize on dollars rather than clicks. Abandoned checkouts trigger CAPI events within seconds, and dynamic ads return the guest to the exact zone, date, and price they left. Lookalike audiences are seeded from your highest-LTV bookers. Megatix does offer analytics and social pixels for campaign tracking; the question to press in any demo is depth — server-side events, per-booking revenue postback, abandoned-cart recovery — because that's where paid-social economics are actually won.

Mobile is the other pressure point: 82% of Clubtech bookings happen on a phone after 10pm. Sub-second load, four-tap checkout, no app install — the mobile experience is built for the guest deciding from the sofa on Thursday night.

Where each platform fits

  • Choose Megatix if your revenue is genuinely event-shaped: ticketed nights, festivals, concerts, and one-off parties, sold with price tiers, scanned at the door, distributed through promoters and cross-border ticket buyers across Southeast Asia. That's its home ground, and it's built well for it.
  • Choose Clubtech if you're a beach club, day club, or pool venue where furniture, packages, and dayparts are the margin — and paid social is the demand engine. White-label on your domain, Opera PMS for hotel-attached venues, payments from Airwallex to Apple Pay and Google Pay, and front-of-house tools that sync the online map to the floor team.
  • Run both, honestly. A venue can keep a ticket platform for its headline event nights and run its daily deck on a furniture-first system — they solve different jobs and coexist cleanly. Map where your money actually lands: if most of it is daybeds and minimum spends rather than tickets, the deck deserves a platform built for it, and Clubtech handles event ticketing within the same white-label flow when you need it.

Commercials differ in posture too. Megatix doesn't publish pricing, and its fees are structured as booking fees passed to or absorbed by the organiser. Clubtech's model is simple and aligned: no monthly fee, and the platform earns when your online revenue grows — book a demo for a quote built around your venue. Founded in Singapore, supporting venue partners in more than 7 countries, processing $332k in weekly GMV — with FINNS Beach Club in Bali as the named proof: millions of dollars worth of pre-paid bookings each month, in the CEO's own words.

Questions operators ask

What is Megatix?

Megatix is an Australian-owned, self-service event ticketing and event-management platform, headquartered in Perth and active across Australia, New Zealand, and Southeast Asia. Organisers sell tickets from their own white-label site and keep 100% of their customer data. It handles one-off and recurring events, access control and scanning, box-office POS, promoter and affiliate tracking, localized payments including buy-now-pay-later, and high-volume onsales — and it powers ticketing for major festivals and nightlife events across the region. Pricing is not published.

Does Megatix work for beach clubs?

For a beach club's ticketed events — a foam party, a sunset session with a headline act — yes, and it has a real footprint in the Southeast Asian nightlife and beach-club scene. The fit question is the unit of sale: Megatix sells ticket types and price tiers for an event, while a beach club's daily revenue is furniture — a guest picking a specific daybed or cabana on a map, buying a package with add-ons, at a daypart price. That spatial, furniture-first booking is a different job than ticketing.

How is Clubtech different from Megatix?

Clubtech is built around the furniture transaction rather than the ticketed event. Guests pick the exact daybed or cabana on a 3D birds-eye map with 360° walkthroughs, buy tiered packages with stackable add-ons, and prepay — while every booking fires to Meta, Google, and GA4 with revenue posted back and abandoned carts recovered via CAPI. Both platforms are white-label and let you own your guest data; the difference is that Clubtech's core product is the spatial daily deck, not the event ticket.

What does Megatix cost?

Megatix does not publish pricing; its model is booking fees that organisers either pass on to buyers or absorb into the ticket price, so you'll need a quote from their team. Clubtech's model is simple and aligned: no monthly fee, and the platform earns as your online revenue grows — book a demo for a quote built around your venue. Whatever platforms you shortlist, get setup, recurring, and per-transaction costs in writing.

Can I use both Megatix and Clubtech?

Yes — they solve different jobs and coexist cleanly. Keep a ticket platform for headline event nights if that's a real part of your business, and run the daily deck — daybeds, cabanas, tables, packages, dayparts — on Clubtech. Clubtech also handles event ticketing within the same white-label flow, so many venues consolidate onto it once the furniture business is the priority.
