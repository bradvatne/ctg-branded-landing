The Tablelist alternative for venues that sell more than the night

TablelistPro runs the nightclub cheaply and well. Clubtech sells furniture across nightclub, beach, and day clubs with a map-first booking and an ads loop.

Book a Demo See the Platform

A nightclub GM shopping for a table-reservation system usually finds TablelistPro fast, and for good reason. It is purpose-built for the nightclub floor, the pricing is public and cheap, and the logos on the site are real. If your entire revenue picture is one venue, one daypart, and a door that opens at 10pm, that is a defensible short list.

The operators searching for a TablelistPro alternative are usually the ones whose revenue stopped being only the night. A group that runs a nightclub and a beach club and a hotel pool is trying to sell furniture across three dayparts on three different sets of economics — and a tool built to run the nightclub cleanly is not the same tool that pre-sells a daybed at sunset or a cabana at noon.

What TablelistPro does well

TablelistPro is US-rooted nightclub and venue management software, and it is genuinely good at the job it was built for. It puts VIP table reservations, event ticketing, guest lists, a built-in CRM, staff roles, contactless payment, live chat, and embeddable booking widgets in one place. Its "Fast Pass" mobile check-in speeds the door, and its sibling consumer app Tablelist.com gives it a bottle-service demand channel most operator tools do not have. It claims 1,500+ venues across 50+ cities and more than $750M in revenue managed, with credible names attached — Marriott International, W Hotels, and Insomniac.

It also has a real drag-and-drop floor-plan admin view. That matters, so be precise about it: TablelistPro is not map-blind. The floor plan is an operator tool for laying out and managing the room — it is not the guest's buying experience. The guest still books through a widget or a list.

And the price is honest and low. Basic is $99/month for admission and ticketing, Standard is $299/month for VIP tables and reservations, and Deluxe is $499/month for POS integration and live chat — one price regardless of venue count, unlimited staff, with a 30-day money-back guarantee. For a single nightclub that lives or dies on table turns and door volume, that is a strong, transparent, purpose-built choice.

Where TablelistPro stops fitting

The gap is not quality — it is scope. TablelistPro is nightlife-only, and that shapes three things a multi-daypart venue depends on.

  • No daypart economics on shared furniture. A beach club sells the same daybed as a morning session, an afternoon session, and a sunset premium. A day club and a hotel pool price the same lounger differently at noon than at 4pm. TablelistPro is built around the ticketed night, not the same piece of furniture earning three different prices across a day. Clubtech runs beach clubs and day clubs on exactly that arithmetic.
  • The guest doesn't buy on a map. TablelistPro's guest booking is widget- and list-driven. There is no guest-facing 3D birds-eye map where the guest explores zones, watches a 360° walkthrough, and taps the exact table before the price is revealed. Selection is a form, not a storefront. Clubtech's nightclub table booking puts the guest on the map first, so they commit to the spot before they see the number.
  • No ads pipeline or white-label in the public product. There is no documented server-side ads loop — CAPI events, GA4 revenue postback, lookalike seeding — and no white-label on the venue's own domain in the public materials. It is also US-centric, with no SEA or Mediterranean presence and no regional payment rails like Midtrans for QRIS, GoPay, or OVO.

None of this makes TablelistPro a bad product. It makes it a nightclub product, sold to venues that are only a nightclub.

Where Clubtech is different

Clubtech is one platform across nightclub, beach club, day club, and hotel pool — reservations, front-of-house operations, and marketing in one system rather than three stitched-together tools, white-labelled on the venue's own domain, with the venue owning its guest data.

  • Map-first selection, prepaid packages. Guests choose the exact table, cabana, or bed on a 3D birds-eye interactive map with 360° walkthroughs, then buy tiered packages — bed only through ultimate — with bottles, cakes, and transfers stacked as one-tap add-ons. Prepayments, deposits, and minimum spends turn the promised table into a guaranteed one, which is how the no-show table stops eating the weekend.
  • A guest-data-to-ads loop. Every booking fires to Meta, Google, and GA4 in real time with its revenue value posted back, so campaigns optimize on dollars, not clicks. Abandoned checkouts trigger CAPI events within seconds and dynamic ads return the guest to the exact zone, date, and price. Lookalikes are seeded from your highest-LTV bookers. And because the booker invites their group and each guest supplies their own details, the venue captures the whole party — not just the payer.
  • Built for the phone at night. 82% of bookings happen on a phone after 10pm — sub-second load, four-tap checkout, no app install, Apple Pay and Google Pay. It sits on top of your existing POS and PMS with no major IT changes, so the switch is additive, not a rip-and-replace.

The honest way to weigh this against a $299/month price tag is not a feature checklist. It is yield. A $299/month tool that doesn't pre-sell the deck can cost more than it saves — a month of no-show tables and un-recovered abandoned checkouts outruns the subscription line by a wide margin. The prepaid-package yield plus the ads-revenue loop is where the real money moves, and neither is a line item you can compare at $99, $299, or $499.

Comparing the commercials fairly

TablelistPro's pricing is public and cheap, and that is a genuine strength: Basic $99/month, Standard $299/month, Deluxe $499/month, unlimited staff, 30-day money-back. If you want a known monthly number for a single nightclub, that is easy to sign off.

Clubtech is priced differently on purpose: no monthly fee, a 4% online processing fee paid by the customer, and a $2,000 one-time setup fee per venue covering widget setup, configuration, and staff training. There is no recurring software line to carry when the floor is quiet, and the model is aligned to online revenue rather than to a subscription. Founded in Singapore, processing $332k in weekly GMV, with venue partners in more than 7 countries. FINNS Beach Club is the named proof — CEO Beau Whittington: "We have grown from simple on-the-day bookings with no financial guarantee… to now having millions of dollars worth of pre-paid bookings each month." Questions operators ask

What is the best TablelistPro alternative for a beach or day club?

TablelistPro is built for nightlife, so a venue selling furniture across dayparts — beach clubs, day clubs, hotel pools — needs a platform built for that unit of sale. Clubtech is one system across nightclub, beach club, and day club, with daypart and demand pricing on shared furniture, a guest-facing 3D booking map, and prepaid packages. That combination is what a multi-daypart operator is usually missing when they search for a Tablelist alternative.

Is Clubtech cheaper than TablelistPro?

It depends on how you count. TablelistPro is a clear monthly fee — $99, $299, or $499 — while Clubtech has no monthly fee, a 4% processing fee paid by the customer, and a $2,000 one-time setup per venue. The sharper question is total cost: a subscription that doesn't pre-sell your tables can lose more to no-shows and abandoned checkouts than it charges, so compare recovered revenue and prepaid yield, not just the subscription line.

Does TablelistPro have a floor plan or booking map?

Yes — TablelistPro has a drag-and-drop floor-plan admin view, and it should not be described as map-blind. The distinction is who uses it: that map is an operator tool for laying out and managing the room, while guests book through a widget or list. Clubtech puts an interactive map in front of the guest, so they choose the exact table or zone and commit before the price is revealed.

Can Clubtech run a nightclub too, or only beach clubs?

Clubtech runs nightclubs alongside beach clubs, day clubs, and hotel pools, with VIP table booking on the interactive map, guest-list and door management, native event ticketing with tiered tickets and QR-code check-in, and prepaid minimums. The advantage for a group is one platform across every venue and daypart rather than a separate nightclub tool. See nightclub management software for the full front-of-house side.

Do we have to replace our POS to switch?

No. Clubtech sits on top of your existing POS and PMS with no major IT changes — it adds the booking, revenue, and marketing layer rather than replacing the stack. The $2,000 one-time setup covers widget setup, configuration, and staff training, so the change is additive and the floor team keeps working the way it already does.
