Clubtech is an all-in-one venue management and booking platform for venues that sell furniture, zones, and dayparts — beach clubs, day clubs, nightclubs, and hotel pools. Online reservations, front-of-house operations, and marketing run in one system: guests pick the exact daybed or table on a 3D map and prepay, the floor team works from the same live inventory, and every booking feeds the campaigns that bring the next one. Most venue booking software was built for a different room: covers, time slots, function sheets. A beach club sells a $450 daybed that has to be resold the moment it walks at 2pm; a nightclub sells the same booth at three prices across one night. That inventory — furniture, zones, dayparts — is what every layer of Clubtech is shaped around, and it is why the three pillars below only work properly as one platform.

Online reservations — guests buy the exact spot

The reservation pillar starts with Clubtech's signature differentiator: the 3D birds-eye interactive booking map. Guests explore the venue's real zones, open 360° walkthroughs, and select the exact piece of furniture — this daybed, that booth — before a price ever asks for commitment. They are no longer buying "a sunbed"; they are defending a spot they have already pictured themselves in.

  • Tiered packages ladder every zone from bed only to party package to ultimate, with stackable add-ons — bottles, cakes, transfers — sold as one tap inside the same flow.
  • Dynamic pricing keeps a peak Saturday and a quiet Tuesday from clearing at the same number, and "Book Online & Save" gives guests a reason to commit days ahead instead of gambling on the walk-up.
  • Prepayment arrives with the booking, so a confirmed party is revenue in the bank, not a promise to test at the door.
  • Events and ticketing with QR check-in run on the same inventory, and when a date sells out, priority-entry capture takes the guest's details anyway — sold out stops meaning lost.

This is the mechanic that took FINNS Beach Club, in CEO Beau Whittington's words, "from simple on-the-day bookings with no financial guarantee… to now having millions of dollars worth of pre-paid bookings each month." The full journey — map, packages, dynamic pricing, gift cards — is on the booking page.

Front of house — the floor runs on the same map

Most booking tools stop at the confirmation email and hand the service day back to printouts. On Clubtech, the map that sold the daybed is the interactive internal floor plan the team runs the shift from. Online sales sync to it in real time, automatic seating allocation places parties, and the operator console shows the day's position before doors open: which zones are prepaid, which furniture needs a push, who arrives when.

Guest-list and visitor management lives in the same system, so the door works from one screen instead of a clipboard and five phones — and every walk-in and list entry lands in the same guest database as the online bookings. There is no re-keying between the sales channel and the service floor, and no version of the truth that expired an hour ago.

How allocation, live inventory, and the operator console run a service day is covered on the operations page.

Marketing — every booking is a conversion

The third pillar closes the loop. Every booking fires to Meta, Google Ads, and GA4 in real time with the revenue value posted back, so campaigns optimize toward booked dollars instead of clicks. Abandoned bookings trigger CAPI events within seconds, and dynamic ads return the guest to the exact zone, date, and price they left. Lookalike audiences are seeded from your highest-LTV bookers — prospecting modeled on the people who buy the big packages, not the people who like the posts.

Because the platform sees every reservation, it also reads the business: 20+ reports cover revenue by hour, booking lead time, product mix, origin markets, and repeat share — guest intelligence without a CSV export in sight. The whole revenue loop, from pixel to posted-back value, is on the intelligence page.

The connection between the three pillars is the product. The map that sells the spot is the floor plan that seats the party, and the booking it produces is the signal that trains the next campaign. Stitch three separate tools together and each handoff loses data; run one platform and the data compounds.

White-label — your domain, your design, your guest data

Clubtech is white-label venue booking software: the journey runs on the venue's own domain, in the venue's own design, and every guest record belongs to the venue. That stance matters more than it sounds. A marketplace puts a brand between you and your guest, takes a commission on demand you often generated yourself, and keeps the relationship — the comparison with the marketplace model is laid out in the ResortPass alternative page.

Data capture goes deeper than the booker. The main booker invites the group, and every guest shares their own details before arrival — so a party of eight grows the marketable database by eight, not one. The audience you retarget, the lookalikes you seed, the repeat behavior you read: all of it is built on data the venue owns outright.

Integrations — on top of the stack you already run

Clubtech sits on top of the venue's existing POS and PMS rather than replacing them, with no major IT changes. Integrations include Opera PMS, Airwallex, Midtrans (QRIS, GoPay, OVO), Apple Pay, Google Pay, Meta Ads, Google Ads, GA4, and WhatsApp.

That shape keeps the switching cost low: the systems your accounting, service, and hotel teams already trust keep doing their jobs, while Clubtech takes over the layer they were never built for — selling furniture, zones, and dayparts online, running the floor against that inventory, and feeding the ad platforms with real revenue.

Mobile-first — built for the group-chat hour

82% of bookings happen on a phone after 10pm. The plan for Saturday is made in a group chat on Thursday night, and the venue whose checkout survives that moment wins the weekend. Clubtech's booking flow is built for exactly that window: sub-second load, four taps from map to confirmation, no app install between the impulse and the payment.

Every second of load time and every extra form field at 11pm is a party that drifts back to the chat and lands somewhere else. This is not a responsive afterthought on desktop software — the phone is the primary venue for the transaction, and the journey is engineered for it.

Multi-currency and payment methods

Premium venues sell to whoever is in town, and payment is where international demand quietly leaks. Clubtech supports multi-currency payments, with Apple Pay and Google Pay for the two-tap wallet crowd, card processing through Airwallex, and Midtrans for Southeast Asian methods — QRIS, GoPay, and OVO.

A guest in Bali pays the way Bali pays; a visitor booking the same venue from abroad pays the way they do at home. Either way the prepayment clears inside the booking flow, in the market's own methods, before the guest ever reaches the door. The vertical pages under solutions show how this plays per market, from Bali to Dubai.

Two more pieces complete the picture. Rollout is a staged program with a dedicated account lead — onboarding, build, training, go-live, and a 90-day optimize phase — detailed on the delivery page. And the commercials are deliberately aligned: no monthly fee, a 4% online processing fee paid by the customer, and a $2,000 one-time setup per venue, broken down on the pricing page.

Questions operators ask

What is venue management software?

Venue management software runs the commercial side of a venue in one system: the inventory guests can book, the reservations and payments against it, the floor plan the team works from on the day, and the guest data every visit produces. For beach clubs, day clubs, and nightclubs the test is specificity — it should manage daybeds, tables, and zones across dayparts, not covers and time slots.

What does an all-in-one venue booking platform include?

Three connected layers. Online reservations: an interactive venue map, tiered packages with add-ons, dynamic pricing, and prepayment. Front of house: a live floor plan, automatic seating allocation, real-time inventory, and guest-list management. Marketing: every booking posted to Meta, Google Ads, and GA4 with revenue attached, plus 20+ reports. On Clubtech these are one platform, so the booking, the floor, and the campaign share the same data.

Can Clubtech run on our own domain?

Yes. Clubtech is white-label: the booking experience runs on the venue's domain, in the venue's design, and every guest record belongs to the venue. There is no marketplace between you and your guest — no commission taken on your own demand, no competitor listed beside your inventory — and the database you build stays yours.

What payment methods does Clubtech support?

Clubtech supports multi-currency payments with Apple Pay and Google Pay, card processing through Airwallex, and Southeast Asian methods through Midtrans — QRIS, GoPay, and OVO. Guests pay in the method that matches their market, and prepayment is collected inside the booking flow, so the revenue is banked before arrival.

Does Clubtech replace our POS?

No. Clubtech sits on top of the existing POS and PMS stack rather than replacing it, with no major IT changes — integrations include Opera PMS. It takes over the booking journey, the live floor plan, and the marketing loop, while the systems your service and accounting teams already run keep running.
