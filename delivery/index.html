Built around your venue

How a rollout actually runs.

Most operators aren’t afraid of new software. They’re afraid of the six weeks in the middle — the half-migrated inventory, the floor team running two systems on a sold-out Saturday. No one-size-fits-all package: a beach club, a nightclub, and a hotel pool don’t sell the same way, and the rollout reflects that — see solutions by venue type.

01

Onboarding

A working session on how the venue makes money — inventory, systems, and the commercial model — before a single screen is configured. Scoping first means the platform is shaped around your model, not the software’s.

02

Build

The 3D birds-eye booking map is built for your actual floor, packages laddered the way you priced them — with dynamic pricing, Book Online & Save spreads, and events with QR check-in. White-label, on your domain, feeding your guest database.

03

Training

Every team a booking passes through — reservations, floor on the live floor plan, door, and marketing — trained by role and included in setup. On the first live service day, nobody is meeting the system for the first time.

04

Go-live

Launched with a lead, not a ticket queue. The same person who scoped the venue is on with real-time support as the first online bookings land and the first prepaid parties hit the floor plan.

05

Optimize

90 days of hypercare plus strategic data advisory: 20+ built-in reports — revenue by hour, lead time, product mix, origin markets, repeat share — and the guest intelligence layer turns the booking log into decisions.

Switching cost

What actually changes — and what doesn’t.

The reason venues delay replacing a booking system is rarely the new software — it’s the imagined IT project around it. On Clubtech, that project mostly doesn’t exist: the platform sits on top of your existing POS and PMS rather than replacing them.

  • No major IT changes — integrations include Opera PMS, Airwallex, Midtrans (QRIS, GoPay, OVO), Apple Pay, Google Pay, Meta Ads, Google Ads, GA4, and WhatsApp, with multi-currency support throughout
  • No monthly fee — the platform earns when your online revenue grows
  • Setup covers widget setup, system configuration, and staff training
  • The honest switching inventory: one new console for your team, a booking widget on your website, guests paying before they arrive

Your POS, your PMS, and your service flow stay put.

FAQ

Questions operators ask.

How long does it take to implement a booking system?

+

It depends on the venue — inventory size, systems, and how much of the commercial model needs building. Clubtech structures every rollout the same way: onboarding, build, training, go-live, then a 90-day optimize phase, with one dedicated account lead through all five stages. Because the platform sits on top of your existing POS and PMS, there is no IT project to schedule around.

What does setup cover?

+

Setup covers widget setup, system configuration, and staff training — the build and training stages of the rollout. There is no monthly fee. The platform earns when the venue’s online revenue grows, which keeps the incentive on our side pointed at your conversion.

Do we need to change our POS or PMS to use Clubtech?

+

No. Clubtech sits on top of the existing stack rather than replacing it, with no major IT changes. Integrations include Opera PMS, Airwallex, Midtrans (QRIS, GoPay, OVO), Apple Pay, Google Pay, Meta Ads, Google Ads, GA4, and WhatsApp, with multi-currency support. Your teams keep the systems they know; Clubtech handles the booking, prepayment, and guest-data layer.

Who trains the venue team?

+

Your dedicated account lead runs training as stage three of the rollout, and it is included in setup. Sessions are structured by role: the reservations team on managing bookings, the floor team on the live floor plan and seating allocation, the door on guest-list and visitor management, and marketing on the ads and reporting workflow.

What support does a venue get after go-live?

+

Go-live is staffed by your dedicated account lead with real-time support, followed by 90 days of hypercare. Alongside it comes strategic data advisory: reading the venue’s booking data with you to improve conversion, using the platform’s 20+ built-in reports — revenue by hour, lead time, product mix, origin markets, repeat share — without a CSV export in sight.

How do we switch from our current booking system?

+

Start with the onboarding stage: your account lead scopes the venue’s inventory, systems, and commercial model, then Clubtech is configured alongside what you run today. Because it integrates with your existing POS and PMS rather than replacing them, switching is a configuration exercise, not an IT migration — and staff training is built into the setup before go-live.
