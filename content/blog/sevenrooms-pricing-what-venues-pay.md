You search "SevenRooms pricing," expecting a page with tiers and monthly numbers. You don't find one. That's not an oversight — SevenRooms does not publish standard list pricing. It's quote-based, and you get a number by contacting sales and describing your business.

For an operator trying to budget, that's frustrating. You can't compare a quote you don't have yet against a subscription you can read on a page. This article does two things: it explains, honestly, what shapes an enterprise reservations quote like SevenRooms', and it shows what a fully transparent alternative looks like when the number is printed before you talk to anyone.

## Why there's no SevenRooms price list

Enterprise reservations platforms almost never post a single number, and there's a real reason for it. Their customers range from a single restaurant to a global hotel group running hundreds of rooms. One list price can't fit both, so the price is assembled per account.

SevenRooms is a strong product in its category — a genuinely good dining CRM. It keeps rich diner profiles, visit history, and tags; it handles deposits, card holds, and prepaid upgrades; and venues keep the guest data captured through its white-label widget. Since its acquisition by DoorDash (announced May 2025, a $1.2B deal), it also sits next to real consumer demand distribution at DoorDash-app scale. None of that comes at one flat price, because none of those customers are the same size.

## What actually shapes an enterprise reservations quote

You won't get a SevenRooms number from us — we don't have one, and inventing one would be worthless to you. But the levers that move a quote like this are well understood. Expect the number to be built from:

- **Number of venues and rooms.** Pricing usually scales with locations, and often with the number of distinct service areas or dining rooms inside each one.
- **Module tier.** Reservations, table management, marketing automation, and add-on capabilities are typically packaged in tiers. The more modules you switch on, the higher the tier.
- **Contract length.** Multi-year commitments frequently carry different rates than a shorter term. Ask what the number looks like at 12 months versus 36.
- **Add-ons.** Extra features — advanced marketing, integrations, premium reporting — are often priced on top of the base tier.
- **Onboarding and support.** Implementation, configuration, training, and the level of ongoing support can be one-time fees, recurring fees, or both.

Read any quote for those five things explicitly. A single headline figure hides whether you're paying per venue or per room, what's in the tier versus bolted on, and what happens to the rate when the contract renews.

## The ownership question worth asking

Here's a question, not an accusation, for any operator weighing SevenRooms: where does your guest relationship ultimately live?

SevenRooms is now a DoorDash company. The guest CRM that made it valuable now sits inside a consumer delivery marketplace that unifies guest data across DoorDash's channels. For many restaurants, that's a feature — more demand, more distribution. For a venue that has spent years building a direct relationship with its highest-value guests, it's worth asking who the guest relationship serves when the platform underneath it is owned by a marketplace. There's no wrong answer, but it belongs on your evaluation checklist, next to the price.

Honestly: we can't tell you a figure, because SevenRooms doesn't publish one. Its pricing is quote-based and custom — you contact their sales team and receive a number built around your business. Expect it to scale with the number of venues and rooms, the module tier you choose, contract length, add-ons, and onboarding and support.

Enterprise reservations platforms serve everything from one restaurant to a global hotel group, so a single list price can't fit. They assemble the number per account instead. It's a normal enterprise-software pattern, not a red flag — just plan for a sales conversation rather than a self-serve checkout.

### What questions should I ask to read a SevenRooms quote?

Ask whether you're billed per venue or per room, exactly which modules are in the tier versus priced as add-ons, what the rate is at 12 months versus a multi-year term, and whether onboarding and support are one-time or recurring. Those four answers turn a single headline figure into a comparable total cost.

SevenRooms is an excellent dining CRM and works well for multi-room restaurant groups.
