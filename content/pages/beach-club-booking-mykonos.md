Mykonos compresses a year of beach club revenue into a handful of months, and it does it with some of the most expensive daybeds in the world. Psarou, Paraga, Super Paradise, Kalo Livadi — the island's inventory is small, the prices are extreme, and the guests planning around them are booking the bed the way they book the villa: in advance, from abroad, as the anchor of the trip. A beach club booking system for Mykonos isn't about volume. It's about making sure furniture this valuable is never sold by DM thread, never walked at 2pm, and never priced on a guess.

## Ultra-premium inventory deserves an ultra-deliberate sale

When a front-row daybed carries a four-figure minimum spend, the booking is a considered purchase — and the flow should sell like one. On Clubtech, guests explore your venue on an interactive map, open a 360° walkthrough of the exact zone, and commit to *their* bed with the minimum spend attached at booking, prepaid. By checkout they aren't comparing venues anymore; they're protecting the centerpiece of the trip.

The mechanics matter more here than in any volume market:

- **Prepayment protects irreplaceable dayparts.** A bed that walks on an August Saturday in Mykonos is revenue the season cannot give back. Money down means the guest arrives or the revenue stays with you — and minimum spends are enforced by zone, committed before anyone reaches the sand.
- **Package ladders match how this island actually sells.** Bed only, party package, ultimate experience — with stackable add-ons like bottles and transfers chosen at checkout. When the booking is the trip's big day, the upgrade path is where the margin lives, and it converts best at the moment of booking, not on the deck.
- **Dynamic pricing prices the calendar honestly.** Late July and a mid-June Tuesday are not the same product. Pricing to demand earns more from the same furniture across a season too short to leave anything on the table.
- **Sold out is a capture, not a goodbye.** With inventory this scarce, your best zones will sell out — often days ahead. Priority-entry capture takes the guest's details anyway, turning a full deck into a retargeting audience and next week's bookings instead of a closed door.
