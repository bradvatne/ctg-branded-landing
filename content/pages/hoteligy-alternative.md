Hoteligy and Clubtech can both make pool inventory bookable. Their scope is different. Hoteligy is a broad hotel guest-experience platform spanning guest information, service booking, requests, ordering, and sunbed booking. Clubtech is a booking, revenue, operations, and marketing platform for premium venue inventory, including hotel pools.

Choose by the job the property needs done.

## Best fit for Hoteligy

Hoteligy is a strong fit when the hotel wants one digital layer for the in-stay guest journey. Its current official site lists sunbed booking alongside restaurant, spa, room service, activities, requests, check-in, signage, kiosks, apps, and smart-TV experiences.

For a resort digitizing many guest touchpoints at once, that breadth is valuable. Sunbed booking is part of a larger guest-experience program rather than a standalone acquisition and revenue engine.

## Clubtech vs Hoteligy at a glance

| Decision area | Clubtech | Hoteligy |
|---|---|---|
| Primary job | Sell and operate bookable pool furniture for residents and outside day guests | Digitize the broader hotel guest experience, including sunbed booking |
| Guest selection | 3D birds-eye map, exact furniture, zones, and 360° walkthroughs | Sunbed booking inside the hotel guest-experience ecosystem |
| Revenue controls | Packages, add-ons, prepayments, deposits, minimum spends, and dynamic pricing | Confirm current pool pricing and upsell controls in the product demo |
| Acquisition | White-label direct booking with revenue-valued advertising events | Primarily an in-stay guest-service layer; confirm external acquisition flow |
| Operations | Live pool inventory, internal floor plan, and allocation | Broad hotel service and content management |
| Commercials | No monthly fee; 4% online fee paid by the customer; $2,000 one-time setup | Confirm current pricing directly with Hoteligy |

## Where Clubtech fits

Clubtech fits when the pool must become an intentional commercial channel. A resident or outside guest selects the exact cabana or daybed, chooses a package, prepays, and arrives against inventory already visible to the floor team. The venue keeps the booking on its own domain and can send its value to Meta, Google, and GA4.

The hotel route is explained on [Clubtech for hotels](/for-hotels/) and the specific workflow on [hotel pool booking](/solutions/hotel-pool-booking/). Confirm PMS and payment requirements during technical discovery; availability can vary by property and market.

## Coexistence and switching considerations

These products can coexist. Hoteligy can remain the digital concierge and in-stay service layer while Clubtech runs the commercial booking engine for cabanas, daybeds, and outside day passes.

Before deciding, map four boundaries: who can book, where payment occurs, which system owns inventory, and which system writes back to the PMS or operating stack. Do not assume folio posting or a specific connector behavior until both vendors confirm it for the property.

## Questions operators ask

### Does Hoteligy offer sunbed booking?

Yes. Sunbed booking is listed as part of Hoteligy's current guest-experience platform.

### Is Clubtech a replacement for every Hoteligy module?

No. Clubtech is not positioned as a hotel-wide digital concierge. It is the stronger fit when the commercial problem is selling, pricing, operating, and attributing premium venue inventory.

### Can both run at one property?

Yes. The cleanest split is Hoteligy for the broad in-stay experience and Clubtech for pool-commerce inventory, provided integrations and ownership boundaries are defined.
