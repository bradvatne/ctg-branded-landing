SevenRooms and Clubtech both help venues own direct bookings and guest data. They start from different operating units. SevenRooms is strongest around restaurant reservations, waitlists, CRM, marketing, and service. Clubtech is built around selling exact daybeds, cabanas, tables, zones, packages, and dayparts before the guest arrives.

The right choice depends on what the venue sells most profitably.

## Best fit for SevenRooms

SevenRooms is a strong fit for restaurants and hospitality groups that need branded reservations, waitlists, table management, rich guest profiles, CRM, marketing automation, and multi-channel booking. Its current official product also supports events, experiences, and prepaid upgrades.

For dining-led operators whose teams use preference and visit history to drive repeat business and personalized service, SevenRooms belongs on the shortlist.

## Clubtech vs SevenRooms at a glance

| Decision area | Clubtech | SevenRooms |
|---|---|---|
| Center of gravity | Exact furniture, zones, packages, dayparts, floor operations, and attribution | Restaurant reservations, waitlists, table operations, CRM, and marketing |
| Guest booking | 3D birds-eye map with exact-furniture selection and 360° walkthroughs | Branded reservation flow by availability; experiences and upgrades can be sold online |
| Revenue setup | Packages, add-ons, prepayments, deposits, minimum spends, and dynamic pricing | Events, experiences, prepaid upgrades, and reservation controls |
| Guest data | Every-guest capture plus booking and revenue behavior | Detailed restaurant guest profiles, preferences, visit history, and POS-linked data |
| Marketing | Revenue-valued bookings to Meta, Google, and GA4; abandoned-booking events | CRM segments, email marketing, and automated guest engagement |
| Commercials | Scoped to the venue and confirmed after discovery | Quote-based; evaluate the current proposal and included modules |

## Where Clubtech fits

Clubtech fits when furniture is not an internal table assignment but the product the guest chooses and buys. Front row, pool edge, shade line, and sunset zone can carry different value. The guest sees that difference on a 3D map, commits to the exact spot, adds the package, and the reservation lands on the operating floor.

That workflow is shown under [Bookings](/platform/#booking) and the [beach club solution](/solutions/beach-clubs/). The attribution layer is under [Marketing](/grow/#ads).

## Coexistence and switching considerations

A resort or hospitality group can use SevenRooms for restaurants and Clubtech for the beach club, pool, day club, or nightclub furniture. This is often cleaner than forcing one booking model over every outlet.

If evaluating a switch, compare the same inventory and buying moment. Ask how guests choose zones, how packages and minimum spends work, what the host or floor team sees, which guest data is captured, and how the booking value reaches marketing systems. ## Questions operators ask

### What is SevenRooms best at?

Restaurant reservations, waitlists, operations, guest CRM, and marketing. Its current product also includes events, experiences, and prepaid upgrades.

### Does SevenRooms own the venue's guest data?

SevenRooms markets branded, commission-free reservations and venue-controlled guest relationships. Review the contract, export terms, and connected channel rules for the exact deployment.

### When is Clubtech the better fit?

When the venue sells unequal physical inventory and needs guest-facing map selection, packages, daypart pricing, live floor allocation, and booking attribution.
