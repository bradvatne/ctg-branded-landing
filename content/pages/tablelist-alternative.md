TablelistPro is a focused nightclub and bar management platform. Clubtech also runs nightclub tables, guest lists, and ticketing, but extends the same furniture-led model into beach clubs, day clubs, and hotel pools.

The choice comes down to venue scope, guest buying flow, and how much of the revenue and marketing chain needs to sit in one system.

## Best fit for TablelistPro

TablelistPro is a strong fit for nightclubs and bars that need VIP reservations, tickets, guest lists, staff tools, CRM, widgets, and an operator floorplan. Its current official pricing is public: Basic at $99 per month, Standard at $299, and Deluxe at $499, with plan-specific features and custom options for seasonal or one-off operations.

That transparency and nightlife focus make it a defensible choice for a single-purpose venue.

## Clubtech vs TablelistPro at a glance

| Decision area | Clubtech | TablelistPro |
|---|---|---|
| Venue scope | Beach clubs, day clubs, nightclubs, and hotel pools | Nightclubs, bars, lounges, events, and hospitality groups |
| Guest booking | 3D birds-eye map with exact-furniture selection and 360° walkthroughs | Website widgets for tables, tickets, and reservations |
| Floor operations | Guest map tied to live inventory, allocation, and operator floor plan | Interactive drag-and-drop operator floorplan and reservation workflow |
| Revenue setup | Packages, add-ons, prepayments, deposits, minimum spends, and dynamic pricing | VIP reservations, deposits, admissions, tickets, and plan-dependent tools |
| Marketing | Every-guest capture plus revenue-valued Meta, Google, and GA4 events | CRM, customer profiles, analytics, and data exports |
| Commercials | No monthly fee; 4% online fee paid by the customer; $2,000 one-time setup | $99, $299, and $499 monthly plans shown publicly; verify current inclusions |

## Where Clubtech fits

Clubtech fits when the same operator sells furniture across nights and dayparts, or wants the guest to choose the exact spot visually before paying. A VIP table, beach cabana, and pool daybed can run through one booking, package, floor, guest-data, and attribution model.

## Coexistence and switching considerations

Coexistence can work across separate concepts, but running the same nightclub table inventory through both platforms creates double-booking and reporting risk. Pick one source of truth for reservations, deposits, guest lists, and table status.

For a switch, compare data export, floorplan setup, staff permissions, promoter workflows, deposits, check-in, and the guest-facing table-selection experience. Price should be measured against the complete workflow, not only the monthly software line.
