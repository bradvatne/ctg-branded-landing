If you run events in Bali, Phuket, or anywhere across Southeast Asia, you've probably crossed paths with Megatix — it moves tickets for some of the region's biggest festivals and nightlife brands. But a beach club or day club has two businesses running under one roof. There's the occasional ticketed event — the foam party, the sunset session with a headline DJ — and there's the daily business of selling furniture: daybeds, cabanas, minimum spends, and packages to a guest who wants a specific spot in the sun this Saturday. Megatix is built for the first. The venues searching for a Megatix alternative are usually trying to solve the second — and discovering that a ticket engine and a furniture engine are not the same machine.

## What Megatix is

Megatix is an Australian-owned, self-service event ticketing and event-management platform, headquartered in Perth and strong across Australia, New Zealand, and Southeast Asia — Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Vietnam. It powers ticketing for large festivals and nightlife events, from Djakarta Warehouse Project to the Perth Royal Show, and it has a genuine footprint in the region's beach-club and nightclub event scene.

Its positioning is sharp and, frankly, good: Megatix is a technology provider, not a marketplace. Organisers sell tickets from their own site through a white-label booking engine and legally own 100% of their customer data — a real differentiator against the incumbents that rent your audience back to you. The feature set is a mature ticketing stack: one-off and recurring events, access control and scanning through the Megatix app, box-office POS, affiliate and promoter tracking, localized payments including buy-now-pay-later, high-volume onsale handling for surge drops, and integrations for analytics, CRM, and resale.

For selling entry to an event — a night, a festival, a one-off party — Megatix is a strong, purpose-built choice. This page is for the venues whose core product isn't a ticket.

## Tickets and tiers vs furniture and dayparts

The unit of sale shapes the whole product. Megatix grew up selling entry to an event: ticket types with price tiers, recurring sessions, high-volume onsales, scan-in at the door. When a beach club uses it, the venue becomes an event — you buy a ฿1,800 or ฿25,000 ticket to *the foam party*, not a specific lounger by the water.

A beach club or day club transaction inverts that. Most of the money isn't a ticket to an event — it's a guest, found on Instagram, deciding on a phone screen whether a specific daybed is worth $450 this Saturday afternoon. What that guest needs is a storefront, not a ticket queue:

- **A map the guest drives.** On Clubtech, guests open a 3D birds-eye interactive map, explore zones, watch 360° walkthroughs, and tap the exact bed or cabana they want. Emotional commitment happens before the price appears — which is why premium rows sell first online instead of being allocated at the door.
- **A package ladder, not a ticket tier.** Bed only, bed plus minimum spend, party package, ultimate experience — with bottles, cakes, and transfers stacked at checkout as one-tap add-ons. Upsell revenue is captured while the card is out, 24/7.
- **Dayparts and demand pricing.** Morning and afternoon sessions on the same furniture, sunset premiums, event-day rates — dynamic pricing so the same deck earns more on Saturday than Tuesday. The [day club model](https://www.clubtechglobal.com/solutions/day-club-booking-system/) runs entirely on this arithmetic.
- **Prepayment as the default.** Deposits and prepaid zone minimums days ahead, killing no-shows and turning the weekend from a walk-up guess into pre-sold certainty — plus priority-entry capture when furniture sells out, so a full deck still grows the database.

Megatix will happily sell tickets to a beach club's event nights, and it does that well. But there is no spatial floor plan where a guest picks unit by unit, no cabana-by-cabana inventory, no bottle-service table reservation running as the daily engine. That's the gap a furniture-first venue feels most.
