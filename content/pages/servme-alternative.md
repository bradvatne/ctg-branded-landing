servme and Clubtech overlap across direct bookings, guest data, payments, and operations. The useful distinction is the transaction at the center. servme is built around restaurant and hospitality reservation management. Clubtech is built around exact premium furniture, zones, packages, dayparts, and the service-day floor.

Both deserve a fair product demonstration.

## Best fit for servme

servme is a strong fit for restaurants, F&B groups, hotels, and entertainment venues that need direct booking channels, table management, guest CRM, marketing, and reporting. Its current official FAQ describes commission-free online bookings, website and social booking channels, POS-linked guest profiles, and support for standalone and multi-location operators.

For a restaurant-led operation that values guest recognition, table control, and re-engagement, that is a credible regional and global proposition.

## Clubtech vs servme at a glance

| Decision area | Clubtech | servme |
|---|---|---|
| Primary transaction | Exact daybeds, cabanas, tables, zones, packages, and dayparts | Restaurant and hospitality reservations across direct and listing channels |
| Guest selection | 3D birds-eye map with exact-furniture selection and 360° walkthroughs | Customizable booking widgets and table-management workflow |
| Revenue setup | Packages, add-ons, prepayments, deposits, minimum spends, and dynamic pricing | Booking rules, payments, CRM, and POS-linked guest spend; confirm package depth |
| Service day | Online inventory, internal floor plan, automatic allocation, and operator console | Reservations, waitlist, walk-ins, table management, and guest profiles |
| Marketing | Revenue-valued bookings to Meta, Google, and GA4; abandoned-booking events | Built-in CRM and email/SMS marketing |
| Commercials | Scoped to the venue and confirmed after discovery | Zero commission on online bookings; confirm subscription and implementation terms |

## Where Clubtech fits

Clubtech fits when the guest must buy the physical spot, not only reserve a cover. A beach-club or day-club guest compares zones, selects the exact furniture, adds a package, prepays, and arrives against a live floor plan. The booking also becomes a revenue event in the venue's advertising and analytics accounts.

See the [Clubtech booking workflow](/platform/#booking), [front-of-house operations](/platform/#operations), and the [restaurant solution](/solutions/restaurants/) for experience-led dining inventory.

## Coexistence and switching considerations

Coexistence can work when servme remains the restaurant reservation and CRM system while Clubtech handles beach-club, pool, or experience inventory. Assign one source of truth per bookable area and decide how guest profiles move between systems.

For a switch, test the whole guest and operator journey: direct channel, exact inventory, deposits, waitlist, allocation, POS boundary, data export, and campaign attribution. Confirm integration behavior against the actual property stack rather than a general logo list.

## Questions operators ask

### Does servme support direct, commission-free bookings?

Yes. Its current official FAQ states that it charges no commission on online reservations and supports direct website and social booking channels.

### Is servme only for restaurants?

No. servme states that it serves restaurants, multi-location F&B groups, entertainment venues, and hotels with F&B operations.

### When is Clubtech the better fit?

When the venue needs guest-facing exact-furniture selection, spatial pricing, packages, service-day allocation, and booking-to-ad attribution in one workflow.
