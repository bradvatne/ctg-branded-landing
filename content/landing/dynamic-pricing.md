## A flat rate leaves money on both ends

Charge one price all week and you undercharge on the nights everyone wants in, and overcharge on the nights you need to fill. Clubtech prices the way premium venues actually trade: the same furniture flexes with demand, so a peak-Saturday daybed and a quiet-Tuesday daybed are not the same product at the same price.

## What flexes

**Demand.** Rates rise as a date fills, so the venue captures the value of a night people are competing to get into.

**Daypart.** Sunset is not lunch. Pricing reflects the parts of the day guests will pay a premium for.

**Inventory.** As the best spots sell, pricing adjusts across what is left — protecting yield without leaving the floor half-empty.

## Paired with early commitment

Dynamic pricing works alongside "Book Online & Save": guests who commit and pay ahead lock a better rate, which pulls demand forward and gives the venue a read on the night days in advance. The result is more revenue from the same seats, and fewer of them sitting empty.

## Questions operators ask

### How does dynamic pricing decide the rate?

Rates flex by demand for a date, the daypart being booked, and the inventory still available. The same furniture earns more when a night is in demand and stays sellable when it is not.

### Will guests be put off by prices that change?

Premium guests already expect peak nights to cost more. Pairing dynamic pricing with early-commitment "Book Online & Save" rewards guests who book ahead with a better rate, so the incentive is to commit early rather than wait.

### Does dynamic pricing replace my own judgment?

No. It applies the pricing logic a revenue manager would want across every date and daypart automatically, so the venue is not hand-adjusting rates night by night to capture the same value.
