## One booking, four chances to earn

A booking is not a single transaction. It is a sequence: the guest commits, adds to the order, pays a rate set by demand, and — if they drift — gets pulled back. Most venue software only sees the first step. Clubtech is built around all four, so the same reservation earns more without a bigger door spend.

## The four levers

**Pre-paid bookings.** Early-commitment pricing — "Book Online & Save" — pulls demand forward. Prepayments, deposits, and minimum spends turn a soft reservation into banked revenue and give the floor a capacity picture days ahead. A daybed sold on Wednesday is not a daybed hoping for a walk-up on Saturday.

**Upsells and add-ons.** Bottles, cakes, transfers, and packages stack inside the booking flow, so guests build a bigger order before they arrive. The upsell runs 24/7 without a staff member having to ask.

**Dynamic pricing.** Rates flex by demand, daypart, and remaining inventory, so the same furniture earns more on the nights it is worth more — and stays sellable on the nights it is not.

**Abandoned-booking retargeting.** A guest who leaves mid-checkout is not gone. Clubtech fires an abandoned-booking event within seconds, and dynamic ads return them to the exact zone, date, and price they were looking at.

## What it adds up to

Revenue is captured at every touchpoint, not just at the point of booking. That is the difference between a booking widget and a revenue platform.

> We have grown from simple on-the-day bookings with no financial guarantee to now having millions of dollars worth of pre-paid bookings each month.

## Questions operators ask

### How does Clubtech grow revenue without raising prices?

By capturing revenue in more places. Prepayment secures the booking, add-ons raise the order value, dynamic pricing earns more on peak dayparts, and retargeting recovers guests who abandoned checkout. Each lever compounds on the same demand you already have.

### What is "Book Online & Save"?

Early-commitment pricing. Guests who book and pay ahead get a better rate than walking up, which pulls revenue forward, reduces no-shows, and hands the floor a capacity forecast days before the shift.

### Do deposits and prepayments reduce no-shows?

Yes. When a guest has money committed to a specific spot on a specific date, the incentive to show up — or to cancel early enough for the venue to resell — is built in. That is the point of collecting payment inside the booking flow rather than at the door.
