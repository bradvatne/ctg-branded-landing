## The door is where the night is won or lost

A slow, uncertain door undoes a great booking flow. Clubtech runs check-in on the same live inventory that sold the spot, so the person scanning at the door sees exactly what was booked and ticketed — no separate list, no guesswork, no queue built on confusion.

## QR check-in on real inventory

Every ticket and booking carries a QR code. Staff scan it at the door, and the live count updates against what was sold. The number on the screen is the number that paid.

## The guest list on one screen

Reservations, ticket holders, and guest-list entries land in one view, so the team works from a single source of truth instead of reconciling a printed sheet with a phone with a host stand. Visitor management and the door are part of the same operating loop as the floor.

## Questions operators ask

### How does QR check-in work?

Each booking and ticket carries a QR code. Staff scan it at the door and the live count updates against the inventory that was sold, so entry is fast and the door count stays accurate.

### Does the door see reservations and tickets together?

Yes. Reservations, ticket holders, and guest-list entries appear in one view, on the same live inventory the floor runs on — not a separate exported list.

### Is check-in part of the same platform as booking?

Yes. Check-in runs on the same platform and inventory as reservations and events, so what sells online is what the door sees.
