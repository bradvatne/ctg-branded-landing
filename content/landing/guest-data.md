## Every booking is a data point you own

A reservation is not just a sale. It is who booked, when, from where, for how much, and how far ahead. Clubtech captures that on every booking and turns it into an asset the venue owns outright — not a report locked inside a marketplace that rents you your own guests.

## Read the business without a spreadsheet

Clubtech ships more than 20 reports across the guest, the operation, and the revenue. Revenue by hour. Lead time by daypart. Average value by package. Repeat-customer share. Origin markets. It is the picture a general manager wants on a Monday and a revenue manager wants before pricing the next weekend — segmented and trending, with no CSV export in the loop.

## The whole party, not just the payer

Because the booker invites their group and each guest supplies their own details, the marketable database grows with every reservation. A table of eight is eight known guests, not one — and they all belong to the venue.

## Owned, not rented

This is the anti-marketplace stance. There is no brand between you and your guest, no commission on demand you generated, and no competitor listed beside your inventory. The database you build is yours to market against for as long as you run the venue.

## Questions operators ask

### What reports does Clubtech include?

More than 20, spanning the guest, the operation, and revenue — including revenue by hour, lead time by daypart, average value by package, repeat-customer share, and origin markets. They are read inside the platform, not exported to a spreadsheet.

### Who owns the guest data?

The venue. Clubtech is white-label: every guest record belongs to the venue, on the venue's own domain, with no marketplace taking a cut of the relationship.

### Does Clubtech capture data on everyone, or just the person who paid?

The whole party. The booker invites their group, and each guest supplies their own details, so a single reservation can grow the marketable audience by the full size of the group.
