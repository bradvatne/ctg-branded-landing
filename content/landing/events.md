## Events on the same inventory as everything else

A big night is not a different business. It is the same floor, the same guests, and the same data — sold a different way. Clubtech runs events and ticketing on the platform that already handles your reservations, so a ticketed night and a daybed Saturday share one inventory, one guest database, and one report.

## What you get

**Tiered tickets.** Sell general admission, tables, and packages side by side, priced the way the night is actually sold. Add-ons stack in the same flow as the ticket.

**QR-code check-in.** Every ticket carries a QR code scanned at the door, so entry is fast and the count on the door screen matches the count that was sold.

**Priority-entry capture when you sell out.** A sold-out event is not a lost guest. When tickets run out, Clubtech still captures contact details for priority entry — so the demand you could not seat this time is demand you can market to next time.

**Every guest, not just the buyer.** The person who buys invites their group, and each guest supplies their own details. A table of eight grows the marketable database by eight.

## Why it matters

When ticketing lives inside the booking platform, the guest who bought a ticket in June is the same known guest you can retarget for the daybed in July. Bolt-on ticketing tools give you a headcount. Clubtech gives you the guest.

## Questions operators ask

### Can Clubtech handle both reservations and ticketed events?

Yes. Reservations and events run on the same platform and the same live inventory, so a venue can sell daybeds one day and a ticketed event the next without a second system or a second guest database.

### How does check-in work on the door?

Each ticket carries a QR code. Staff scan it at the door, and the live count updates against the inventory that was sold — the same floor view the team uses for reservations.

### What happens when an event sells out?

Clubtech captures priority-entry contact details after inventory runs out, so sold-out demand becomes marketable guest data rather than a turned-away crowd.
