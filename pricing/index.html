No monthly fee

Most booking software is sold as a subscription: a fixed fee, invoiced monthly, due before the first booking arrives and due again whether the online channel had a record week or a washed-out one. The venue carries the risk; the vendor's next real incentive is the renewal date.

Clubtech removed that line from the invoice. There is no subscription and no recurring platform fee — the platform's commercial engine is the online processing fee, which only exists when an online booking exists. That puts both parties on the same side of the P&L: the only way the platform grows is by making the venue's online channel convert better — faster checkout, smarter packages and pricing, ads that optimize toward booked revenue.

It isn't a theoretical model. It runs today at $332k in weekly GMV across venue partners in more than 7 countries.

The 4% online processing fee

Online bookings carry a 4% processing fee — paid by the customer at checkout, not by the venue. It is added on the guest's side of the transaction, so it never appears as a line on the venue's bill.

The fee attaches to the channel the platform builds: online prepayment. That's where a $450 daybed stops being a maybe and becomes banked revenue days before the visit, where every guest in the party enters the database, and where each booking fires to Meta, Google Ads, and GA4 with its value attached. A fee that only exists when a booking exists scales down as naturally as it scales up — a quiet Tuesday generates no platform cost at all.

The one-time $2,000 setup

Getting a venue live is charged once: $2,000 per venue. It covers three things:

  • Widget setup. The booking widget live on your site, under your brand.
  • System configuration. Your floor mapped, inventory and zones loaded, packages and pricing built around what the venue actually sells.
  • Staff training. The reservations, floor, door, and marketing teams trained on the system they'll run every service.

That fee is the entry cost — the full rollout it belongs to, from onboarding through build, training, go-live, and a 90-day optimization period with a dedicated account lead, is laid out on the delivery page.

Subscription pricing vs the aligned model

To be fair to the subscription model: it is predictable. A flat SaaS fee is easy to budget, easy to approve, and familiar to any finance team — and for some venues that predictability is genuinely worth something.

The trade-off is where the risk sits. A fixed fee is due in the months the online channel underperforms, in the low season, and in the ramp-up period before the system has proven anything. The vendor is paid the same either way, so the pressure to keep improving conversion after go-live is reputational, not financial.

The aligned model inverts that. With no monthly fee, the venue's fixed cost for the platform is zero after setup — and because the 4% processing fee is paid by the customer, the venue isn't carrying a percentage either. The platform only earns when online bookings happen, so its incentives point at the same numbers the venue watches: conversion rate, average booking value, prepaid revenue on the books before the weekend. Questions operators ask

How much does Clubtech cost?

Clubtech has no monthly fee. There are two numbers: a 4% processing fee on online bookings, paid by the customer at checkout, and a one-time $2,000 setup per venue covering widget setup, system configuration, and staff training. There are no subscription tiers and no other charges in the model — the platform earns as your online revenue grows.

Does Clubtech charge a monthly fee?

No. There is no subscription and no recurring platform fee. That is a commercial decision, not a promotion: a fixed monthly fee gets paid whether online bookings grow or not, while Clubtech's model ties the platform's earnings to the venue's online revenue. If bookings don't move, the venue isn't writing a check for software it isn't using.

Who pays the 4% online processing fee?

The customer. The 4% online processing fee is added on the guest's side of the transaction at checkout, so the venue is not billed for it. It applies to online bookings — the channel where prepaid revenue, guest data, and ad attribution are created in the first place.

What does the $2,000 setup fee cover?

It is a one-time fee, charged per venue, covering three things: setup of the booking widget, configuration of the system around your inventory and packages, and training for your staff. The wider rollout — onboarding through go-live and a 90-day optimization period — is described on our delivery page.

Are there per-booking or hidden fees?

The commercial model has exactly three parts: no monthly fee, a 4% online processing fee paid by the customer, and a one-time $2,000 setup per venue. Those are the only numbers in the model — no venue-paid per-booking charges, no feature tiers, and no usage bands layered on top.
