Success story · Luna Beach Club · Bali

Over 1 billion rupiah in online bookings.
In fifteen days.

Luna Beach Club runs its daybeds, sunbeds and cabanas on Clubtech — booked and paid under Luna's own brand, on Luna's own map. Here's what a single fortnight of live demand actually looked like.

Product insights · 26 Jan – 9 Feb 2026
%

of booking revenue came from pre-reserved furniture — daybeds, sunbeds and cabanas, paid for before arrival.

%

of furniture bookings were premium tiers. Given the choice on the map, guests reliably trade up.

%

of every booking was for a single day — Saturday. The engine absorbs the peak with no queue at the door.

%+

of bookings were made the same day as the visit — walk-up demand, captured as paid, tracked reservations.

The week, by demand

Every day sells.
Saturday sells hardest.

Luna takes bookings seven days a week — there is no dead night. But more than one in five land on Saturday, and Saturday guests spend the most per booking. Share of all bookings, by the day they're booked for:

Weekends (Fri–Sun) drove more than half of all bookings — and an even larger share of revenue.

How guests book

Two kinds of guest.
One booking flow.

The same engine handles the group planning a month out and the guest deciding on the sand this afternoon — and charges both before they sit down.

The planners

Booked weeks ahead

Premium deck sofas and large daybeds — the statement furniture for a big day out — are reserved and paid for well in advance, some up to a month before arrival. Luna's most valuable inventory is committed long before the weekend.

Top premium furniture: booked ~4 weeks out
The walk-ups

Booked the same day

Entry sunbeds and loungers move on the day itself — spontaneous, in-the-moment demand that used to walk up to a host stand. The flow is quick enough that guests self-serve and pay in seconds, so every walk-up becomes a tracked, paid reservation.

Typical entry lounger: booked same day
What the fortnight showed

Three things the
data made obvious.

53%

of Saturday's guests booked on Saturday itself — and the vast majority within the final three days. Short-fuse demand, captured.

Thu

Thursday is the busiest pre-pay day of the week. Guests lock in and pay for the weekend mid-week, before they arrive.

7/7

Bookings landed on all seven days. No dead night — the platform monetises the quiet weekdays as well as the peaks.

The product, on the floor

Guests pick the exact spot.
Then they pay for it.

Luna's booking map: guests explore the venue from above, tap the exact daybed, sunbed or cabana, add to it, and commit before they arrive. The same engine runs the floor on the day — check-ins, the seat map, and the live view of what's booked before doors open.

One venue. One fortnight. Over a billion rupiah in bookings — every one of them paid up front, tracked, and owned by Luna.
